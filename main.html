About Albania

Albania, or Republika e Shqipërisë (Land of the Eagles) in Albanian, is a developing nation in Southeastern Europe known for its unique culture, mountainous terrain, Adriatic and Ionian coastline, and a complex modern history of isolation and transition. It has recently gained international attention for appointing the world's first cabinet-level AI minister, Diella.

Key Facts
Capital & Largest City: Tirana
Official Language: Albanian (an independent branch of the Indo-European language family)
Currency: Albanian Lek (ALL)
Population: Approximately 2.7 to 3.1 million people (estimates vary)
Religion: The country is a secular state with a Muslim majority, and significant Orthodox and Catholic Christian minorities. Religious tolerance is a strong cultural element.
Government: Unitary parliamentary republic
Membership: Member of NATO since 2009 and an official candidate for European Union (EU) accession.
Culture and Society
Albanian culture is highly distinctive:
Besa: A cultural code of honor emphasizing hospitality and protecting guests.
Language & Identity: The national symbol is the black double-headed eagle, a legacy of the 15th-century national hero Skanderbeg, who led a rebellion against the Ottoman Empire.
Bunkers: The landscape is dotted with hundred of thousands of concrete bunkers built during the isolationist communist regime of Enver Hoxha (1944-1985), some of which have been repurposed into museums and hotels.
Communication: A unique aspect of local non-verbal communication is that a head shake often means "yes", and a nod means "no", which can be confusing for visitors.
