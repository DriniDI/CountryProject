Welcome to Albania!

Explore the rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant history of Albania.

1000 window town

The "1000 Window Town" in Albania is Berat, a historic city famous for its stunning Ottoman-era architecture featuring white houses with many large windows that cascade down hillsides, earning it UNESCO World Heritage status

Kruja Castle

Kruja Castle (Kalaja e Krujës) in Albania is a historic medieval fortress, the symbol of Albanian resistance, home to national hero Skanderbeg, offering museums (Skanderbeg Museum), stunning mountain views, and proximity to the Old Bazaar was built around the 5th/6th century

Gjirokastër Castle

Gjirokastër Castle, a massive historic fortress in southern Albania, is a significant UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its extensive grounds, museums (including military and ethnographic exhibits), and a captured US Air Force jet. Visitors explore its history, enjoy panoramic views of the Drini Valley, see ancient cannons, and discover its past as a prison, making it a key cultural landmark in Albania's "Stone City".

Dajti Park

Dajti Park (Dajti Mountain National Park) near Tirana, Albania, is a popular natural escape with hiking, cable car access (Dajti Ekspres), adventure parks, restaurants, and wildlife like bears and wolves, offering stunning views of the city and Adriatic Sea, perfect for day trips with activities for all ages. Dajti Ekspres Cable Car: A 15-minute ride providing panoramic views as it ascends 1,050 meters (3444.88 Feet) to the summit.

White Drin/i

The river measures approximately 285 kilometers (177 miles) within Albanian territory, although its total length, measured from the source of its longest tributary (the White Drin in Kosovo), is about 335 kilometers (208 miles). The Drin is formed by the confluence of its two headwaters, the Black Drin and the White Drin, near the town of Kukës in northeastern Albania.

Rozafa Castle

Rozafa Castle (Kalaja e Rozafës) is a historic fortress near Shkodër, Albania, famous for its strategic location, stunning panoramic views of Lake Shkodër, the Buna & Drin rivers, and the Albanian Alps, plus the enduring legend of Rozafa's sacrifice

Fun fact: three brothers building a fortress whose walls kept collapsing until an old man revealed they must sacrifice the wife who brought them food the next day, leading to the youngest brother's wife, Rozafa, being walled in alive, but with her right breast, eye, hand, and foot left exposed to nurse, see, feed, and rock her infant son, symbolizing immense sacrifice for the greater good(this is not a myth btw)
